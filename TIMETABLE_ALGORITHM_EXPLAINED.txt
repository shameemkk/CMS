===========================================================
   TIMETABLE GENERATION ALGORITHM - BEGINNER EXPLANATION
===========================================================

Written for: Beginners with no prior algorithm knowledge
Based on: CMS Project (backend/src/controllers/timetable.controller.js)


-----------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS THE PROBLEM WE ARE SOLVING?
-----------------------------------------------------------

Imagine you have:
  - A list of SUBJECTS (e.g., Math, Physics, Lab)
  - A list of TEACHERS (each assigned to a subject)
  - 5 DAYS per week (Mon - Fri)
  - 5 TIME SLOTS per day (periods 1 to 5)

You need to fill in a timetable grid like this:

          | Period 1 | Period 2 | Period 3 | Period 4 | Period 5 |
  --------|----------|----------|----------|----------|----------|
  Monday  |   ???    |   ???    |   ???    |   ???    |   ???    |
  Tuesday |   ???    |   ???    |   ???    |   ???    |   ???    |
  ...     |   ...    |   ...    |   ...    |   ...    |   ...    |

RULES you must follow:
  1. A teacher cannot teach TWO classes at the SAME time.
  2. Every subject must get its required number of hours per week.
  3. Lab subjects should be placed in back-to-back (consecutive) slots.
  4. Special subjects (minor/major) get reserved/priority slots.

This is the problem. The algorithm below solves it.


-----------------------------------------------------------
THE TIME SLOTS (Fixed Schedule)
-----------------------------------------------------------

Each day has exactly 5 periods:

  Period 1: 09:30 - 10:30
  Period 2: 10:30 - 11:20
  Period 3: 11:30 - 12:30   (after a short break)
  Period 4: 13:30 - 14:30   (after lunch)
  Period 5: 14:30 - 15:30

So the full timetable grid = 5 days x 5 periods = 25 slots total.


-----------------------------------------------------------
BIG PICTURE: HOW THE ALGORITHM WORKS (3 STEPS)
-----------------------------------------------------------

STEP 1: TRY to generate a timetable (up to 10 times)
STEP 2: Keep the BEST result from all attempts
STEP 3: Report any subjects that did not get enough hours


Let's go deeper into each step.


-----------------------------------------------------------
STEP 1: GENERATE ONE TIMETABLE ATTEMPT
-----------------------------------------------------------

The main function is called generateTimeSlots().
It loops through every day and every period in that day.

  FOR each day (Monday to Friday):
    FOR each period (1 to 5):

      A) Is this a PRIORITY SLOT for a special subject (minor/major)?
         --> If YES, try to place that special subject here.
         --> If that subject has no hours left, mark slot as RESERVED.

      B) Otherwise, pick the BEST normal subject to place here.
         --> Use findSuitableSubject() to choose.

      C) Check: Is the chosen subject's teacher ALREADY teaching
         at this time in another class?
         --> If YES, skip. Do not place it. Leave slot empty.
         --> If NO, place it! Record teacher as busy at this time.

After the loop, you have a filled (or partially filled) timetable.


-----------------------------------------------------------
HOW THE ALGORITHM PICKS A SUBJECT (findSuitableSubject)
-----------------------------------------------------------

When we reach an empty slot, we need to decide WHICH subject to place.
The algorithm SORTS subjects by priority:

  Priority 1 (highest): Subjects with MORE remaining hours needed
                         (they are behind schedule -- place them first)

  Priority 2: Among equal, prefer subjects already scheduled LESS
              (balance the workload)

  Priority 3: Among equal, prefer subjects with MORE total hours
              (important subjects get priority)

  Special case for LABS:
    Labs prefer consecutive slots (period 3 & 4 together, etc.)
    If consecutive slot is NOT available, skip for now.

  Special subjects (minor/major):
    They are NOT picked during normal scheduling.
    They are only placed in their PRIORITY slots (handled separately).

  Randomness:
    If multiple subjects have the exact same priority score,
    the algorithm RANDOMLY picks one. This is why running it
    multiple times gives different results.


-----------------------------------------------------------
HOW TEACHER CONFLICTS ARE PREVENTED
-----------------------------------------------------------

The algorithm keeps a record called "teacherSchedule" (like a diary).

  teacherSchedule[teacherID][day][period] = true/false

Before placing a subject in a slot:
  CHECK: Is teacherSchedule[teacher][day][period] already TRUE?
    --> YES? Teacher is busy. Skip this subject for this slot.
    --> NO?  Teacher is free. Place subject. Mark as TRUE.

Also: Teachers from ODD semesters (1,3,5,7) share a conflict pool.
      Teachers from EVEN semesters (2,4,6,8) share another.
      This prevents a teacher from teaching two different classes
      at the same time.


-----------------------------------------------------------
STEP 2: TRY 10 TIMES, KEEP THE BEST
-----------------------------------------------------------

Because of randomness, each attempt gives a different timetable.
The algorithm runs up to 10 attempts and keeps the BEST one.

What is "best"?
  1. Fewest missing subject hours (lowest deficit)
  2. If deficit is equal, more total slots filled

If any attempt achieves ZERO deficit (all hours scheduled perfectly),
it stops early and uses that result immediately.

This is called a "multi-attempt retry with best-result selection."


-----------------------------------------------------------
STEP 3: REPORT MISSING HOURS
-----------------------------------------------------------

After generation, the algorithm checks:
  For each subject:
    Required hours per week = X
    Actually scheduled hours = Y
    Deficit = X - Y

If deficit > 0, a WARNING is returned to the user:
  "Subject [Math] is missing 2 hours this week."

The admin can then manually edit the timetable to fix it.


-----------------------------------------------------------
SUMMARY: THE ALGORITHM IN PLAIN ENGLISH
-----------------------------------------------------------

1. Loop through every slot (25 total: 5 days x 5 periods).

2. For each slot:
   a. If it is a reserved priority slot -> place special subject.
   b. Otherwise -> find the subject most "behind schedule"
      that doesn't cause a teacher conflict.
   c. Place it. Mark teacher as busy.

3. Repeat this whole process up to 10 times.

4. Keep the attempt with the fewest missing hours.

5. Warn the admin about any subjects still missing hours.


-----------------------------------------------------------
TYPE OF ALGORITHM
-----------------------------------------------------------

This is called a:

  GREEDY ALGORITHM   --> At each slot, pick the locally best subject.
  + RANDOMIZATION    --> Random tiebreaking enables different results.
  + RETRY STRATEGY   --> Run multiple times, keep best result.

It is NOT:
  - Genetic Algorithm (no evolution/crossover)
  - Backtracking Search (no undoing previous choices)
  - Integer Linear Programming (no math solver)

It is simple, fast, and practical for small-to-medium timetables.


-----------------------------------------------------------
GLOSSARY (Simple Definitions)
-----------------------------------------------------------

Greedy Algorithm   : At every step, make the choice that looks best
                     RIGHT NOW, without planning far ahead.

Deficit            : The number of hours a subject still needs but
                     hasn't been scheduled yet.

Priority Slot      : A specific period reserved for special subjects
                     (minor/major) configured by the admin.

Consecutive Slots  : Two periods back-to-back (e.g., Period 3 & 4).
                     Labs need this because they are 2 hours long.

Fisher-Yates Shuffle: A standard method to randomly reorder a list.
                      Used here to randomize subject selection.

Teacher Conflict   : When one teacher is assigned to two classes
                     at the exact same time. This is not allowed.
